# Comparative Analysis — Grading Rubric

## Standardized 0–100 Scoring System

All analysis domains use a consistent grading scale:

| Grade | Score   | Meaning |
|-------|---------|---------|
| A+    | 95–100  | Exceptional — industry-leading, exceeds all standards |
| A     | 90–94   | Excellent — meets or exceeds enterprise standards |
| B+    | 85–89   | Very Good — strong with minor refinement opportunities |
| B     | 80–84   | Good — solid foundation, notable improvements possible |
| C+    | 75–79   | Above Average — meets minimum professional standards |
| C     | 70–74   | Acceptable — functional but with significant gaps |
| D     | 60–69   | Below Standard — major issues require attention |
| F     | 0–59    | Failing — critical problems, not production-ready |

## Scoring Principles

1. **Evidence-Based**: Every score must cite specific files, lines, or metrics
2. **Relative + Absolute**: Score against both industry baselines AND peer projects
3. **Weighted Dimensions**: Not all metrics within a domain carry equal weight
4. **Penalty Floor**: No domain drops below 0, even with compounding penalties
5. **Bonus Ceiling**: Innovation and unique capabilities can push scores above 90

## Research Paper Scoring Dimensions

| Dimension | Weight | Criteria |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| Novelty | 20% | Originality of contribution, delta from prior art |
| Reproducibility | 15% | Code availability, dataset access, clear methodology |
| Applicability | 20% | Practical implementation feasibility |
| Evidence Quality | 15% | Statistical rigor, ablation studies, baselines |
| Clarity | 10% | Writing quality, figure quality, structure |
| Impact Potential | 20% | Citation trajectory, adoption by industry |

## Innovation Extraction Scoring

| Dimension | Weight | Criteria |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| Analogical Fit | 25% | How naturally the concept maps to the target domain |
| Implementation Cost | 20% | Effort required (inverse — lower cost = higher score) |
| Emergent Value | 25% | "Free" capabilities unlocked by the integration |
| Novelty Delta | 15% | How different from existing capabilities |
| Risk Level | 15% | Implementation risk (inverse — lower risk = higher score) |
